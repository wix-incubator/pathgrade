Thoughts about Vibe coding and websites
In my mind vibe coding has 3 different use cases:
Empowering developers- the user is a developer and the output can be anything. Examples: Claude code and Cursor
Build an app for everyone- no coding skills are needed and the output is an app.  Examples: Base44, Lovable 
Building a website for everyone- The product must include everything that a professional website needs. Examples: Harmony, Lovable, Wix Vibe
Websites and Apps are very different. 
Application functionality is mostly not defined, the majority of what you can build with vibe coding is relatively simple. The value of the vibe coding is the fact you can build the application you want without the need to know programming.
Website functionality is mostly predefined. It must be ready for production with high performance, reliability and strong security,  legal requirements for privacy (GDPR), cookie banners and even more laws for accessibility etc. 
That is before very complex stuff like payments transactions (E-Comm), scheduling, taxes, and support for SEO. 
When it comes to websites, building a website with stunning visuals is a big concern, to remind everybody this is the number one thing we did at Wix, allowing everybody to design their own websites.When creating an application, in most cases people care less for the look and feel (it needs to look ok, very few care for more).
A few months ago we started a project called Wix Vibe with an ambitious goal to bridge the gap between the ease of vibe coding and reliable infrastructure that is needed for websites. The team did an incredible job and in addition built a great product that sparked a lot of new ideas and innovation across the company. The introduction of headless verticals, the visual editor for vibe coding and the website beautification efforts are some of the big wins they brought us.
Wix is uniquely positioned to combine vibe coding with website creation. Wix Harmony, formerly known as Editor3 launching next month, will bring together the speed of creation of vibe coding, running on Wix’s robust infrastructure, delivering top security, performance, accessibility, and high quality design, and of course the flexibility of visual editing.

This is our vision of how vibe coding (for websites) should work, introducing a UI where humans and AI collaborate seamlessly, without sacrificing quality or reliability.

Thoughts about the future of websites
It is always very hard to predict the future, but I want to try and predict what things will look like two years from now.
Looking ahead, I see two possible scenarios for how the digital landscape may evolve (and it might be that both will coexist).
In the first scenario, traffic will continue to live within websites, but the way users experience them will probably change. To lead this transformation, we’re developing Web5: A concept of a  conversational chat, but instead of being just text it provides a site wide visual experience. Maintaining brand identity and adapting all the new AI values and capabilities, but doing so in a stunning visual way, to deliver the best experience the site owner was aiming for.  This is a very exciting innovative project, where we try to invent the future user interface that leverages AI but also allows for people to create an experience to connect their brand with their customers.
In the second scenario, traffic shifts beyond traditional websites and into LLM-driven experiences. In this model LLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Grok and others) become the future interface, the future browser of the web. In this scenario the content is still hosted on websites (with payment/scheduling/commerce/other backend..  stack) and the LLM just communicates and activates the website. For this to be really successful, the LLM will need to allow the websites to create “content capsules” with the brand design, product visuals, and interactive components that are working inside the LLM.
It will mean that all the backend  is still relevant (probably even more complex as you want the AI to “see” a lot more content), and creating the website visuals to fit the LLM  is also a lot more complex, both mean that the challenge for the personal/small business users becomes much harder and Wix will bring them even a bigger value.
I've been asked a few times about a 3rd scenario, where LLMs are also hosting all the content, I don't believe it is likely because it raises many questions and challenges, for example: How will you upload content to LLMs in this case? How do you edit content? How do you add videos, images? Where do you define your ecommerce rules? tax rules,  payments, scheduling or fulfillment? How do you manage your inventory? If you are a bank, a restaurant? 
This 3rd scenario will require OpenAI to develop all the backends for everything, and for the user to upload all the content to ChatGPT. It is even more complicated because we also have Gemini, Claude, Preplexity, Grok… so the user will have to upload everything to all of them. And then there is the huge question: How are we going to sync things? Like inventory for ecomm? Or scheduling for classes?
I really don't think the AI LLMs vendors will have much value in that, as opposed to the second scenario, where they just replace the interface and still get most of the benefits.
In scenarios #1 and #2 which I believe are likely, Wix will give users a higher value, as the technology required will create a significantly higher complexity and will demand support for a lot of new standards. We can already see some of that starting to happen.
We now need to ensure that our users can be discovered and generate business from LLM traffic. Need to support agentic commerce and business transactions, and to provide our users with a seamless experience in managing their business across these new traffic sources. For this future, we’re already investing in forward-looking innovation projects such as ChatGPT Apps, Websites MCP, ACP, NLWEB, A2A Site Agents, and more.
The way the internet will work in the future is now evolving in front of us, daily and quickly, which is why, reacting super fast and continuing innovation are much more important than ever before. We are of course working also on other innovation projects for forward looking products such as Wix twins, Orion and more

2026 Centralized Efforts

Editor 3 (Wix Harmony)
Owner: Oded  Sponsor: Amit

In the past year, we made a decision to focus on our self-creators audience by building a new editor- a hybrid of AI vibe coding and visual editing. 
A few things make Harmony unique:
A full drag and drop editor combined with vibe website/content creation, so users can work with both at the same time on the same project delivers pixel-perfect design.
Fully integrated AI into the editing process, you can ask the AI to build things, explain things, in the editor and beyond
Full support for security, accessibility, quality, SEO, and performance, things that are a must for a website, but are extremely hard to build with vibe coding alternatives, even harder, full e-commerce, scheduling and transactions
The website you build, works, it is easy to vibe a part of it, or all of it, without breaking the website, this stability, does not exist with any of the alternatives, when you change one thing, nothing else breaks
A component model, because of the builder architecture, you can add new components to the website and have them integrate like native components, but when you edit, the editing are contained inside the component, making it possible to continuously develop functionality without breaking the project/adding bugs like in traditional vibe coding
The combination of those unique advantages make Harmony the most powerful vibe website platform globally 
Like any breakthrough product, especially one as sophisticated as this, there is still more to complete. In 2026, we will learn from how users create with it, and from that, shape the roadmap.

Builder 
Owner: Amit  Sponsor: Avishai

We want Wix websites to be truly open, fully standard  and allow our users to export their websites. This is especially important for customers that want to maintain vendor freedom and has direct implications on hosting setup and security concerns. For these reasons we will continue investing in the builder effort, aiming to reach the following goals in 2026:
Develop editor extensions such as Velo, Verticals & 3rd party code that is compatible with the standard way to develop web content
Easily import new components or develop them with AI
Export projects, and websites and become standard 
The viewer is transforming our runtime rendering model to a build time model, where it will generate fully standard React sites 

We achieved huge and exciting milestones this year as Wix Harmony is built on the Builder infrastructure. This means that the new components and applications model is now complete, and the editor is interacting with its elements using “Builder technology”. It includes advanced editing capabilities (inner elements), standardization (Pure react components), simplification, auto-panels (less to develop, and better consistency) , and import functionality, including AI creation of components. 

Missing elements:
Page and full-site export
Headless components with full drag-and-drop functionality, AI-driven design capabilities
Velo that is modern and standard
